Heritage Sites

Rani ki Vav (Queen's Stepwell)

Rani ki Vav is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is one of the most intricately designed stepwells in Gujarat. It was built by Queen Udayamati in the 11th century in memory of her husband, King Bhimdev I. The stepwell is known for its stunning architecture, including intricate carvings of deities, celestial beings, and mythological scenes.

Location

Champaner-Pavagadh Archaeological Park:

This archaeological park is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site and encompasses the historical city of Champaner and the sacred Pavagadh Hill. The site includes a variety of well-preserved structures, including mosques, temples, fortifications, and tombs, representing a blend of Hindu and Islamic architectural styles. The Jama Masjid and the Kalika Mata Temple are notable structures within the park.

Location

Sabarmati Ashram, Ahmedabad:

Sabarmati Ashram, also known as Gandhi Ashram, is the former residence of Mahatma Gandhi. Located on the banks of the Sabarmati River in Ahmedabad, it served as the epicenter for the Indian freedom struggle. The ashram now houses a museum, showcasing Gandhi's life and teachings, and is a place of reflection and homage.

Location

Dholavira, Kutch:

Dholavira is an archaeological site belonging to the ancient Indus Valley Civilization and is one of the five largest Harappan sites in the Indian subcontinent. It is renowned for its well-planned urban infrastructure, advanced water conservation systems, and unique architecture. Dholavira provides valuable insights into the ancient history and culture of the region.

Location

Sidi Saiyyed Mosque

The Sidi Saiyyed Mosque is known for its intricate stone lattice work, particularly the famous "Sidi Saiyyed Ni Jali" or the Sidi Saiyyed's Grille. Built in 1573, this mosque is a fine example of Indo-Islamic architecture. The lattice work on the windows is exceptionally delicate and is said to have inspired the design of the logo of the Indian Institute of Management, Ahmedabad.
